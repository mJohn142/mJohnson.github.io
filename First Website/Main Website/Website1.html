OverView

Alcoholism/Alcohol abuse/Alcohol dependency is the unhealthy habit of consuming too much alcohol daily. It can cause craving (a strong need to drink), loss of control (not being able to stop drinking once you've started), physical dependence (withdrawal symptoms), and tolerance (the need to drink more alcohol to feel the same effect).

It can lead to serious problems such as car crashes and other accidents; Risky behavior, violent behavior, suicide and homicide. Some of the symptoms are Alcohol abuse(just like Alcoholism except at its infant stage), Alcohol Use Disorder(a medical condition that doctors diagnose when a patient’s drinking causes distress or harm), and Binge drinking(a pattern of drinking that brings blood alcohol concentration (BAC) levels to 0.08 g/dL. This typically occurs after 4 drinks for women and 5 drinks for men—in about 2 hours).

According to the 2015 National Survey on Drug Use and Health (NSDUH), 86.4 percent of people ages 18 or older reported that they drank alcohol at some point in their lifetime; 70.1 percent reported that they drank in the past year; 56.0 percent reported that they drank in the past month.

Families of these students worry about their children who are living on campus due to frat parties and hazing. Especially those who are athletes.

Our Mission

Our organization was founded by our leader Matthew Johnson in the year 2017. He wanted to eradicate Alcoholism in college drinking because he lost his best friend to alcoholism on March 10th, 2015. His friend went to St. John’s and graduated in May 2014. That’s when Matthew had decided to do something about it.

In 2015, 26.9 percent of people ages 18 or older reported that they engaged in binge drinking in the past month; 7.0 percent reported that they engaged in heavy alcohol use in the past month.

CDHC works to prevent the effects of drug and alcohol misuse on college students. We also aim to support, inform and inspire vulnerable and disadvantaged students to help them reach their full potential.

Our organization focuses on three core areas:

1. To inform and educate people about the effects of drug and alcohol misuse on college students.

2. To provide support for those most vulnerable, those at high risk of misuse or disadvantaged through circumstance.

3. To support personal development of disadvantaged students.
