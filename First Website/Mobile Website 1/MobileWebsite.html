Our Mission

Our organization wants to help college students and their families by ensuring them that there children will be in the safest environment. They will be surrounded by our medical staff 24/7. Our facilites have pools, non-alcoholic beverges, spa's, and gym's. Our medical staff will work with those students one on one; identifying what caused them to drink, how they began to drink, and laying out the steps to break out of their addiction. We want those families to feel at ease when they send their child to us because they will know that they are in good hands and that they will be cured.

People who have come to us for help

Our friend here Douglas(the man in the middle) struggled with his Alcohol Addiction. He began drinking around his sophomore year in college. He went to a fraternity party in 2015 and got wasted. Afterwards, he began to drink too much and his life went downwards. He couldn't get a job and he was losing hope; until he decided to call CDHC. At first, Douglas oubted whether we could help him end his addiction to alcohol. After going through the therapies as well as talking to the doctors about treatment, Douglas was able to get rid of his addiction. He is now a high school basketball coach at BerryWood Highschool in New Jersey. Also, he donates and volunteers for our organzation as well as speaks to other students about the dangers of alcohol at our events. He always says that "without the help of CDHC, I would have never been able to send time with my family and friends or even be a coach."

People who have come to us for help

Jane is currently enrolled to the University of Syracuse and she was an alcoholic. She is a senior who is majoring in Communications. She started to drink alcohol around her junior year in college due to her mother passing away at the age of 63. She struggled with her mother's loss and she decided to use alcohol as a way to escape her problems. On January 14th, 2015, one of her friends saw Jane completely drunk in her dorm room. Jane was yelling and throwing bottles on the wall and that's when her friend called public safety. Jane was admitted to the hospital for Alcohol Abuse. Jane realized that she had a problem and contacted us. She was immediately sent to one of our doctors and she gradually received helped. She jogged everyday and didn't touch an alcohloic beverage. Now, she is sober and living good!
